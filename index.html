Fish Tacos

  • Time: 30 min
  • Serves: 4-6
Ingredients Quantity
Medium tomatoes, seeded and finely chopped 2
Small red onion, peeled and finely chopped 1
Roughly chopped cilantro 1/2 Cup
Clove garlic, minced 1
Jalapeño, halved lengthwise, seeded and cut crosswise into half moons (optional) 1
Mayonnaise 1/4 Cup
Sour cream 1/2 Cup
Limes, 1 halved and 1 cut into wedges 2
Kosher Salt
Freshly groun black pepper
Tablespoon canned chipotle pepper, finely chopped (optional) 1
Flour, preferably Wondra or other fine-milled flour 1/2 Cup
Chili powder 1 1/2 Tsp
Milk 1/2 Cup
Peanut oil, plus a splash more for greasing pan 1/4 Cup
Butter 1 Pat
Flounder or any firm white-fleshed fish, cut across the grain of the flesh into strips about 1/2 inch wide by 3 inches long 1 Pound
6-inch fresh corn tortillas 12
Shredded green cabbage 2 Cups

All words used from NYT Cooking

Step 1: Combine the tomatoes, onion, garlic, cilantro and jalapeño (if using).

Step 2: Whisk the mayonnaise and sour cream until combined. Season to taste with the halved lime, salt, pepper and chipotle (if using).

Step 3: Mix together the flour, chili powder and 1 1/2 teaspoons each of kosher salt and black pepper. Pour the milk into another medium bowl, and place the fish into it.

Step 4: Pour 1/4 cup of the peanut oil into a 12-inch frying pan and place over medium-high heat until it shimmers and is about to smoke. Remove the fish pieces from the milk bath and dredge them lightly through the flour mixture, shaking to remove excess. Add the butter to the pan. Place some fish pieces in the oil, without crowding them, and cook until deep golden brown on one side, 3 to 4 minutes. Turn carefully and cook for 1 minute more. Remove to a warmed, paper-towel-lined plate and sprinkle with salt. Repeat with the remaining fish.

Step 5: Meanwhile, lightly grease a skillet with a drizzle of oil and set over medium heat. Heat the tortillas, one or two at a time, until they are soft and hot. Keep them warm, wrapped in a dish towel.

Step 6: Fill each tortilla with 3 pieces of fish, browned side up, followed by tomato salsa and a pinch of cabbage. Drizzle with the cream sauce. Serve 2 to 3 tacos per person, with lime wedges and hot sauce on the side.
